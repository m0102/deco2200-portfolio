Project: AuTour

A tour planning app for use in an autonomous tour bus to help tourists create a personalised schedule and unique experience while travelling.

Introduction

The Brief

‘Map out the interactions that will be required to assist people in interacting with autonomous vehicles in future cities.’

More specifically: How do we create a unique and seamless experience for tourists using an autonomous tour bus.

The Solution

Our solution was to take away the rigidity of pre-planned tour scheduling, allowing users to visit only the places they want, in the order they choose. Users can browse and input their preferences for destinations, length of the tour and other activities. With the inclusion of the autonomous vehicles’ interactions with information sources about traffic and crowding and notifications to allow tourists to reroute and avoid these in advance. Complete with additional features including a map, upcoming stops, choice of travel mode and ability to edit the plan at any point on the journey.

The AuTour tour planning app is a seamless addition to a future autonomous bus which gives users complete control of their experience.

Welcome: A welcome screen prompting users to start their journey.
Categories: A screen showing all the pages of the app with icons (also included in footer for easy navigation). Users can start with ‘Browse’ if they want to see all the possibilities or jump to ‘Plan Trip’ if they already know where they want to go. Once they have created a plan, they can go to ‘Edit plan’ to change it at any time. Dashboard is the main screen they can use to see where they are, quickly change the route or see their next stops.
Browse - Browse All: The ‘Browse’ pages have multiple categories, such as popular and fun activities, which users can scroll through for more specific lists, or ‘browse all’ to see an overview. The ‘browse all’ page has cards for each place/activity which users can add to their plan directly or tap for more info.
Browse - Popular Landmarks: The ‘Popular Landmarks’ screen has information about each place related to the current journey, such as how long it will take to get there and how busy it is currently. Users can see more info or add it to their plan.
Plan Trip: The ‘Plan Trip’ page has key categories for users to personalise and chose, including destinations, activities, and times. The bus then calculates a schedule based on this. They can quickly go back to the browse page to add places from there as well.
Day/Edit Plan: The user’s day plan Is shown with icons and times. They can reorder, remove or add to this. The bus also provides suggestions to add based on their choices and current plan. Once they’re done they are directed to the dashboard.
Dashboard: The ‘Dashboard’ page is a home page for the app, providing a map of where the user is, upcoming stops and options to change these. As well as notifications about traffic and overcrowding which they can respond to by skipping or rerouting the stop if they like.
